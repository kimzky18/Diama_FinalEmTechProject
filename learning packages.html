GROUP 1

A smartwatch is a wearable device that combines the functionalities of a traditional wristwatch with additional features and capabilities typically associated with smartphones and fitness trackers. It is designed to be worn on the wrist and offers various functionalities beyond simply telling time.

GROUP 2

Smart wearables, also known as smart devices or wearable technology, are electronic devices that can be worn on the body or integrated into clothing or accessories. These devices are designed to provide various functions and features beyond traditional accessories.

GROUP 3

A smart health wearable is a type of wearable device specifically designed to monitor and track various health-related metrics and provide insights into an individual's well-being. These devices are often worn on the body, such as on the wrist or as a patch, and utilize sensors and technology to collect data on a person's physical activities, vital signs, and other health parameters.

GROUP 4

Smart clothing, also known as smart textiles or e-textiles, refers to garments or fabrics that are embedded with electronic components and technologies to provide additional functionality beyond regular clothing. Smart clothing combines fashion and technology to create innovative and interactive garments that can sense and respond to the wearer's needs or the environment.

GROUP 5

A 3D environment refers to a virtual space that is created to simulate a three-dimensional representation of the physical world or an imaginary world. It is typically generated using computer graphics and can be explored or interacted with using specialized software or hardware.

GROUP 6

3D modeling is the process of creating a three-dimensional representation of objects or environments using specialized software. It involves the creation, manipulation, and arrangement of digital vertices, edges, and faces to construct a virtual 3D object or scene.

GROUP 7

Holographic printing refers to a printing technique that produces holographic images or designs on various surfaces, such as paper, plastic, or metal. It creates a three-dimensional visual effect that appears to have depth and movement when viewed from different angles or under different lighting conditions.
